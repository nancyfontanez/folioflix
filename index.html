8 websites to learn coding for free

There are many websites that offer free resources for learning to code. Here are a few options that you might find helpful:

1️⃣ Codecademy : Codecademy is a popular platform that offers interactive courses on a variety of programming languages, including Python, Java, C++, and more.

2️⃣ The Odin Project : The Odin Project is a free, open-source curriculum for learning web development. It was created with the goal of helping people learn the skills they need to get hired as web developers. The curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Ruby, and more.

3️⃣ edX : edX is a massive open online course (MOOC) platform that offers a wide range of programming courses from top universities and institutions.

4️⃣ Coursera : Coursera is another MOOC platform that offers a variety of programming courses from universities and companies around the world.

5️⃣ SoloLearn : SoloLearn is a platform that offers interactive courses on programming languages like Python, C++, and Java, as well as web development and data science.

6️⃣ MIT OpenCourseWare : MIT OpenCourseWare is a free and open platform that offers access to MIT course materials, including lectures, assignments, and exams. You can find a variety of programming courses and resources on the platform.

7️⃣ freeCodeCamp : freeCodeCamp is a non-profit organization that offers a comprehensive curriculum of coding tutorials and exercises, as well as the opportunity to work on real-world projects and earn certifications.

8️⃣ GitHub : GitHub is a platform for developers that offers version control, project management, and collaboration tools. It also has a large repository of open-source projects that you can use to learn and practice your skills.

Remember to choose a resource that aligns with your learning style and goals, and be patient with yourself as you learn – coding can be challenging, but it is also very rewarding.

6 tips for scheduling to learn coding

Here are some steps you can follow to set up a schedule for learning to code:

1️⃣ Determine your learning goals: The first step in creating a schedule is to have a clear idea of what you want to accomplish. Consider what you hope to learn, and how much time you can realistically dedicate to learning each day or week.

2️⃣ Identify the best time for learning: Think about when you are most productive and focused, and try to schedule your learning time around those times. Some people find it helpful to learn first thing in the morning, while others prefer to learn later in the day.

3️⃣ Break your learning goals down into smaller tasks: Rather than trying to tackle large, complex projects all at once, it can be helpful to break your goals down into smaller, more manageable tasks. This can help you stay focused and make progress more consistently.

4️⃣ Schedule time for learning and practice: Once you know how much time you have available and what you want to accomplish, you can start scheduling specific blocks of time for learning and practice. It can be helpful to set aside dedicated time for learning each day or week.

5️⃣ Stick to your schedule: It's important to be consistent and make the most of your dedicated learning time. Try to minimize distractions and stay focused on your tasks.

6️⃣ Review and adjust your schedule as needed: As you progress in your learning, you may find that your schedule needs to be adjusted. Don't be afraid to make changes as needed to ensure that your schedule is working for you.

Overall, the most important thing is to be consistent in your learning efforts and to make sure you have enough time to make progress towards your goals. With the right mindset and approach, you can set up a schedule that works for you and helps you learn to code effectively.

How to start the #100daysofcode challenge

The 100 Days of Code challenge is a commitment to practice coding for at least one hour every day for 100 consecutive days. It is a great way to improve your coding skills and build a strong foundation in programming. Here are some steps you can follow to start the challenge:

1️⃣ Choose a programming language: Before you start the challenge, you need to decide which programming language you want to learn or practice. There are many popular programming languages to choose from, such as Python, Java, C++, and more. Consider your goals and interests when selecting a language.

2️⃣ Set a schedule: Determine how much time you can commit to coding each day and set a schedule accordingly. It's important to be consistent and stick to your schedule to make the most of the challenge.

3️⃣ Find resources and set goals: There are many online resources available to help you learn to code, such as tutorials, video courses, and online communities. Find resources that suit your learning style, and set specific goals for what you want to achieve during the challenge.

4️⃣ Start coding: Once you have chosen a language, set a schedule, and found resources, it's time to start coding! Set aside time every day to practice coding and work towards your goals. Don't worry about making mistakes or getting stuck – it's all part of the learning process.

5️⃣ Track your progress: It's helpful to keep track of your progress during the 100 Days of Code challenge. This can be as simple as making a list of the topics you covered each day or keeping a journal of your thoughts and experiences.

Remember, the 100 Days of Code challenge is about consistent practice and improvement. Don't worry too much about the end result – just focus on making progress each day. Good luck!
